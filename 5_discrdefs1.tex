Let $I$ be the set of integers. We will consider cases $I=\Zb \cap [0,+\infty)$ and $I=\Zb.$ Let $\Ac=\{A_k\}_{k\in I}$ be a sequence of linear isomorphisms $\Rb^d\to\Rb^d,$ indexed by numbers from $I.$ Consider homogeneous and inhomogeneous linear systems of equations with this sequence:
\begin{IEEEeqnarray}{rCll}
x_{k+1}&=&A_k x_k,\qquad &k\in I, \label{eq:homogen} \\
x_{k+1}&=&A_k x_k + f_{k+1},\qquad &k\in I. \label{eq:nonhomogen}
\end{IEEEeqnarray}

We denote fundamental matrix of this system by $\Phi_{m,l}.$ We'll use linear subspaces of $(\Rb^d)^{I}$ -- the vector space of sequences of vectors from $\Rb^d,$ indexed by numbers from $I.$ For $\omega\in\Rb$ denote by  $\Nc_{\omega}(I)$ the Banach space of all sequences with bounded norm $\normban{x}_\omega=\sup\limits_{k\in I} \vmod{x_k} (|k|+1)^\omega.$

\begin{deff}
For real $\alpha,\beta$ we say that the sequence $\Ac$ have the \indef{Perron property} $B_{\alpha,\beta}(I)$ if for any sequence $f\in \Nc_\alpha(I)$ there exists a solution of inhomogeneous equation \eqref{eq:nonhomogen} that lay in $\Nc_\beta(I).$ For $\alpha = \beta$ we will write simply $B_\alpha(I).$
\end{deff}
